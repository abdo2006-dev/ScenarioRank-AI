01 — Data Source
Where the
data comes from
All inputs are user-supplied at runtime. No hidden dataset. The system accepts structured candidate profiles, a role definition, and a business scenario — then generates all evaluation data through the pipeline.

⚡
No pre-loaded dataset. Every evaluation is live.
The system generates all criteria weights, scores, risk profiles, and rankings dynamically at runtime. The same pipeline produces a different ranking when the scenario or decision mode changes.

04 — Scenario Agent
Weight vector
calibration
LLM Call Input: baseline weights + scenario Output: shifted normalized weights
For "Rapid scaling in a new geographic market", Claude recommends boosting strategic_scalability and transformation_leadership — since this scenario demands leaders who can operate in unfamiliar territory and build teams from scratch. All weights re-normalize to 100.
Baseline → Adjusted delta applied per criterion
adjusted_i = max(0, baseline_i + delta_i)
normalized_i = adjusted_i / Σ adjusted_j × 100
All weights re-sum to exactly 100 after normalization.

Weighted Fit Score — Math
WFS = Σ ( weight_i × score_i ) / 10
wfs_calculation.txt
// Alexandra Chen — Best Fit calculation strategic_scalability: 22.0 × 7 = 154 transformation_leadership:17.8 × 7 = 124.6 stakeholder_management: 16.0 × 8 = 128 innovation_digital: 12.4 × 7 = 86.8 operational_execution: 11.8 × 8 = 94.4 domain_expertise: 10.7 × 8 = 85.6 crisis_management: 9.3 × 7 = 65.1 ───────── Sum = 738.5 WFS = 738.5 / 10 = 73.85 // Overall confidence (weighted avg): // Σ(conf_i × weight_i) / Σ weight_i = 0.82
server.mjs — computeWeightedFitScore()
function computeWeightedFitScore(scores, weights) { let total = 0 for (const k of Object.keys(weights)) total += (scores[k] ?? 0) * (weights[k] ?? 0) / 10 return Math.round(total * 100) / 100 }

server.mjs — risk computation
// Execution risk: low exec ability = high risk ExecutionRisk = 100 - ( 0.45 × operational_execution × 10 + 0.30 × domain_expertise × 10 + 0.25 × crisis_management × 10 ) // Expected outcome (0-100 scale) EOS = 0.35 × WFS + 0.20 × AdaptabilityScore + 0.20 × (100 - ExecutionRisk) + 0.10 × (100 - CultureRisk) + 0.10 × (100 - TimeRisk) + 0.05 × Confidence × 100 // Risk-adjusted score (ranks lowest-risk mode) RAS = WFS - 0.25 × ExecutionRisk - 0.20 × CultureRisk - 0.15 × TimeRisk - 0.15 × (1 - Confidence) × 100 - 0.10 × (100 - Adaptability) - 0.15 × OpportunityCost
Bias Review (deterministic): Flags any criterion where confidence < 0.65. If 3+ criteria are flagged, recommends human review. Does not change scores — only annotates them.

09 — Impact
Why this
system matters
Most hiring decisions fail not because of bad candidates — but because of evaluation frameworks that weren't designed for the specific decision being made.
01
Trade-offs made explicit
Every recommendation comes with what you gain, what you sacrifice, and what you're optimizing for. Not just a winner — a decision context.
02
Scenario simulation
The same candidate ranks differently under crisis vs growth vs transformation. The system shows where candidates are robust vs fragile.
03
Transparent reasoning
Every score has evidence. Every weight has a justification. Every ranking is reproducible. An auditor can trace any output back to a formula.
04
Leaders as a system
Pair simulation surfaces combinations where complementarity is high but conflict risk is low — insight that individual evaluation cannot provide.
The core insight
"Who is the best candidate?" is only part of the decision.
The harder question is: "Who is the best candidate for this scenario, optimizing for this objective, accepting these risks, at this opportunity cost?"

ScenarioRank AI structures that question so the answer is explainable — not just to the hiring team, but to every stakeholder who has to live with the outcome.
